## ğŸ“Š Understanding Your Numbers

### EMI Breakdown

Your EMI consists of two parts:

1. **Principal Repayment**
   - Portion that reduces your loan balance
   - Increases over time

2. **Interest Payment**
   - Portion that goes to the lender
   - Decreases over time

**Early in Loan:**
- More of EMI goes to interest
- Less goes to principal
- Example: $1,896 EMI = $1,625 interest + $271 principal

**Later in Loan:**
- More of EMI goes to principal
- Less goes to interest
- Example: $1,896 EMI = $271 interest + $1,625 principal

### Interest Rate Impact

**Small Rate Changes = Big Impact**

Example with $300,000 loan over 30 years:

| Rate | Monthly EMI | Total Interest |
|------|-------------|----------------|
| 6.0% | $1,799 | $347,515 |
| 6.5% | $1,896 | $382,633 |
| 7.0% | $1,996 | $418,527 |

**0.5% increase = $100/month more = $36,000 more over 30 years!**

### Accrued vs Total Interest

**Accrued Interest:**
- Interest accumulated so far
- From start date to today
- What you've paid/owe currently

**Total Interest:**
- Interest over entire loan life
- From start to final payment
- What you'll pay eventually

**Example:**
- Loan: $300,000 at 6.5% for 30 years
- After 1 year:
  - Accrued Interest: ~$19,400
  - Total Interest (projected): $382,633
- You've paid 5% of total interest so far

## â“ Frequently Asked Questions

### About EMI

**Q: Why does my EMI change when I update the interest rate?**
A: EMI is calculated based on the current interest rate. When the rate changes, the monthly payment amount changes to maintain the same loan tenure.

**Q: Can I change my EMI?**
A: EMI is calculated automatically. To change it, you would need to adjust the loan amount, interest rate, or tenure.

**Q: Is the EMI shown the exact amount I'll pay?**
A: Yes, it's the calculated monthly payment based on your loan terms. However, your actual payment might include additional fees or charges from your lender.

**Q: Why is my EMI different from what my bank told me?**
A: Small differences can occur due to:
- Different calculation methods
- Processing fees not included
- Rounding differences
- Insurance or other add-ons

### About Accrued Interest

**Q: Why is my accrued interest so high?**
A: Interest accumulates daily on your outstanding balance. Over time, even small daily interest adds up to significant amounts.

**Q: Does accrued interest include interest I've already paid?**
A: Yes, it's the total interest from the loan start date to today, whether paid or unpaid.

**Q: Why did my accrued interest jump after a rate increase?**
A: The system recalculates using the new higher rate. The jump reflects the increased interest cost going forward.

**Q: Can I reduce my accrued interest?**
A: You can reduce future interest by:
- Making prepayments to reduce principal
- Refinancing to a lower rate
- Making extra payments

### About Calculations

**Q: How often do calculations update?**
A: Calculations update:
- Instantly when you change loan details
- Automatically when you update interest rates
- Daily for accrued interest (based on current date)

**Q: Are the calculations accurate?**
A: Yes, we use industry-standard formulas. However, always verify with your lender for official amounts.

**Q: What if I have multiple rate changes?**
A: The system tracks all rate changes and calculates interest for each period separately, then sums them up.
